Willford Family Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

Makes about 3 dozen cookies.

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp. baking soda
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter, softened (salted and it is fine if you don't have unsalted)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 2 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips (I always use Ghirardelli 60% Cacao, which makes them extra delicious)
  1. Preheat oven to 350.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour and baking soda; set aside.
  3. In the bowl of an electric mixer with the paddle attachment, combine the butter and both sugars; mix on medium speed until pale and fluffy.
  4. Reduce speed to low; add the salt, vanilla and eggs. Mix well, about 1 minute.
  5. Add flour mixture; mix until just combined.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop heaping tablespoon-sized balls of dough about 2 inches apart on baking sheets lined with parchment paper (if you have it, or use nonstick spray).
  8. Bake until cookies are golden around the edges but still soft in the centers, 8 to 10 minutes.
  9. Let cool on baking sheet 1 to 2 minutes, then take them off and let them cool completely.
